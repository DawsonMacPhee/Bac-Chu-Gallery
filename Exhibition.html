Introductory write up for exhibition.

Modern Pandemic

The home is a complex place or idea, where opposing forces can intersect: intimacy and remoteness, privacy and openness, safety and danger, power and powerlessness. In this exhibition, the home is examined as a space of tensions that have lately intensified and made the quotidian uncanny and the strange normal. In 2020, the COVID-19 lockdown dramatically altered everyday life.

In painful paradox, sheltering in place to prevent the spread of the virus has increased the threat of domestic abuse. Reports of domestic violence increased during the pandemic, with UN secretary general Antonio Guterres comparing the domestic violence facing girls and women to the battlefield, provocatively evoking language of war.

This exhibition engages with the idea of home and its many iterations, and the complex relationship of individuals to home. In employing peaceful images of home to discuss the dangers associated with home, this exhibit challenges the narrative of home as a safe place and exposes patterns of violence enacted in the home and intensified through the course of the current global pandemic.

We at the Bachinski/Chu express our gratitude to the School of Computer Science for providing the space for this exploration of home and the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic on experiences of dwelling.
